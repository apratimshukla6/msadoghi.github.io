Overview

This new graduate seminar course surveys the recent developments in data management with the primary focus centered around Blockchain covering topics such as storage architecture, concurrency controls, agreement protocols, byzantine fault-tolerant protocols, secure transactions, distributed ledgers, decentralized and resilient infrastructures, and trusted execution.

Workload:

The most important component of the course is a semester-long creative project on topics such as distributed transactions or distributed ledgers. The project must be done in groups* (no limit on the group size), and the role and contributions of each group member must be clearly articulated. All projects must by approved and will be supervised by the instructor. The outcome of the final project will consists of (1) building a prototype system or benchmarking existing platforms and (2) writing a short research paper with the ultimate goal of submitting to a top-tier conference, of course, submission is optional and does not affect your final grade. Additionally, each student is expected to present 1 or 2 papers throughout the semester. Students must also submit their presentation slides and a short written review for each paper they present (similar to the conference-style review). The papers will be selected from the content tab. Students must coordinate with the instructor/TA in advance for the paper they wish to present (we will adopt first come, first served when assigning papers).

* With prior permission from the instructor, the projects can be done indvidually if there is a sufficient justification.

Grading:

The final grade will be based upon the following components:
  • Paper Presentations/Slides/Review: 20%
  • Class Participation: 10%
  • Final Project: 70%
